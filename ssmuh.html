Understanding Your Options

What's Now Possible on Your Lot.

Bill 44 changed the baseline across BC. Under the new legislation, most single-family lots in municipalities with a population over 5,000 can now accommodate up to 4 residential units by right — no rezoning required. Properties located near designated transit corridors may qualify for up to 6 units.

This applies broadly across Lower Mainland municipalities including Pitt Meadows, Maple Ridge, Coquitlam, Burnaby, Langley, and the City of Vancouver. Specific requirements — including setbacks, height limits, and parking — vary by municipality.

We assess your specific lot, confirm what's achievable under current bylaws, and walk you through the realistic development options before any planning dollars are spent.

Common Questions

SSMUH FAQ

SSMUH is a category of residential development established through BC's Bill 44 (Housing Statutes Amendment Act, 2023). It permits property owners to build multiple residential units on lots previously zoned exclusively for single-family homes. The legislation was designed to increase housing supply across BC by enabling duplexes, triplexes, fourplexes, and small multiplexes on standard residential lots without requiring rezoning applications.

Under Bill 44, most single-family lots in BC municipalities with a population over 5,000 can now accommodate up to 3 units for lots under 280 m², up to 4 units for most standard lots, and up to 6 units for properties within 400 metres of a bus stop with frequent transit service. The specific number depends on your lot size, municipality, and proximity to transit. We assess your lot as part of our discovery process.

Most standard single-family lots in Lower Mainland municipalities qualify — the legislation was designed to apply broadly. That said, site-specific factors matter: lot dimensions, existing easements, heritage designations, and flood plain status can all affect what's buildable. Utility connections and servicing capacity are also assessed early. We'll review your property and give you a straightforward picture of your realistic development options.

A duplex in the Lower Mainland typically ranges from $700,000 – $1.2M depending on size, finishes, and site conditions. Triplexes and fourplexes range from $900,000 – $2M+. These figures include design, permits, and construction. Because we work on a fixed-price model, you'll know your total cost before construction begins — no surprises mid-project. We'll give you a realistic budget range during our first conversation.

Multi-unit projects typically take 14–22 months from initial planning through occupancy. The design and permit phase generally runs 4–7 months, and construction takes 10–14 months depending on scope and complexity. We provide a clear project timeline during pre-construction so you can plan around your financing, existing tenants, or personal circumstances.
