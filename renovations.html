Common Questions

Renovation FAQ

A full home remodel typically means significant scope: a kitchen transformation, multiple bathroom renovations, new flooring throughout, structural changes, or a full-floor reimagination. It's not cosmetic surface updates — it's a coordinated, managed transformation that requires permits, trade sequencing, and design integration. If you're unsure whether your project qualifies, tell us what you're envisioning and we'll give you an honest assessment.

Yes. Permit coordination is included in our full-service approach. We manage the permit application, required engineering or structural reviews, and all inspections throughout the project. You never need to navigate municipal processes on your own — that's part of what you're hiring us to handle.

It depends on the scope and phasing of the project. For major whole-home renovations that involve multiple living areas simultaneously, living on-site is often not practical or safe. For more contained scopes, phased construction can sometimes allow partial occupancy. We'll give you an honest, specific assessment during your consultation — not a generic answer that overpromises comfort.

For comprehensive whole-home remodels, expect 6–12 months from project kick-off through final handover, depending on the scope and complexity of structural changes. Permit timelines, which vary by municipality, are also a factor. We provide a detailed project schedule during pre-construction so you know every milestone in advance.

For significant whole-home scope, our target minimum is $500,000. This reflects the management intensity, trade coordination, design services, and material quality that our process requires. Cosmetic or partial-room updates aren't typically within our scope — but if you're investing seriously in your home, we'd love to have that conversation.
