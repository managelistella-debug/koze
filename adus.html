ADU Types

What Can Be Built on Your Property?

BC's recent zoning changes have significantly expanded what's possible on residential lots across the Lower Mainland. What was once prohibited or tightly restricted is now increasingly permitted — and the opportunity for property owners is real.

Depending on your lot size, zoning, and existing structures, you may be eligible for a detached coach home in your backyard, a laneway home (where permitted), an attached secondary suite above a garage or beside the main house, or a basement suite with a separate entrance and full compliance. BC's evolving Small-Scale Multi-Unit Housing (SSMUH) legislation has opened up options that simply weren't available a few years ago.

The best first step is a property assessment. We'll review your lot, your zoning, and your goals — and tell you honestly what's achievable and what the investment looks like.

ADU Options

Types of ADUs We Build

Each property is different. Here are the most common ADU types we design and build across the Lower Mainland.

Detached

Coach Home / Backyard Suite

A fully separate structure in your backyard — completely independent from the main house. Maximum privacy for tenants or family, and typically commands the highest rental rates. Requires adequate lot size and setback compliance.

Attached

Above-Garage or Side Suite

An ADU built above or beside an existing garage, or added as an attached structure to the main home. Efficient use of existing structure, often lower construction cost, and well-suited to lots where a fully detached build isn't feasible.

Interior

Basement or Secondary Suite

A fully legal, permitted secondary suite within the existing home's footprint — typically the basement. Requires separate entrance, proper egress, fire separation, and mechanical compliance. Often the most accessible entry point.

Laneway

Laneway Home (Where Permitted)

A compact, fully independent home built along the rear lane of a property, typically 500–700 sq ft. Available in select municipalities. Increasingly common in Vancouver and surrounding cities as zoning evolves under SSMUH legislation.

Common Questions

ADU & Coach Home FAQ

We build all common ADU types: detached coach homes and backyard suites, attached above-garage or side suites, laneway homes (where zoning permits), and interior secondary suites. The right type for your property depends on your lot size, zoning designation, setback requirements, and goals. We assess all of this during the feasibility phase — and give you an honest picture of what's actually possible.

ADU costs vary significantly by type, size, and site conditions. A basement suite conversion may start from $150,000–$250,000; a detached coach home typically ranges from $350,000–$600,000+. These are directional figures — accurate pricing requires a site review and defined scope. We provide specific cost guidance during the feasibility phase, and a fixed-price proposal once design is complete.

Timeline depends on the ADU type and permitting timelines in your municipality. A secondary suite interior conversion may take 3–5 months. A detached coach home or laneway home typically takes 8–14 months from design through occupancy. Municipal permit wait times vary — we factor this into your project schedule during pre-construction so you have clear milestones from the start.

We manage the application, required drawings, structural or engineering reviews, and all required inspections throughout construction. Building without permits creates serious liability at resale and may result in required demolition. A properly permitted ADU is the only kind we build.
